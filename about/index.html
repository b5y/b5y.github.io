My career path Link to heading

My journey into computer science is quite typical. I developed an interest in mathematics and physics during my secondary school studies. After graduating from the Presidential Physics and Mathematics Lyceum 239, I continued my education at one of Russia’s most prestigious and renowned faculties—the Mathematics and Mechanics Faculty of Saint Petersburg State University, which is among the top universities in the country.

At some point, I realised that software engineering was quite boring for me due to the lack of mathematics in my work. I intended to apply for a double-degree master’s program at ITMO University and University of Amsterdam. After applying for exchange studies at Aalto University, I secured a Research Assistant position in the Probabilistic Machine Learning Group under the supervision of Distinguished Professor Samuel Kaski. I had always wanted to try myself as a teacher and was honored to be a Teaching Assistant for the Machine Learning: Basic Principles course taught by Alex Jung. Since then, I have decided to stay in Finland.

My master’s thesis at Aalto University (Machine Learning, Data Science and Artificial Intelligence) focused on medical image analysis using the NaViT model.

I am passionate about machine learning and generative AI.
